Summary

CISA, in coordination with the Department of Energy (DOE) and FBI, is releasing this joint Cybersecurity Advisory (CSA) to warn organizations in the energy sector about ongoing cyber operations by an advanced persistent threat (APT) actor we track as VOLTZITE. This threat actor has demonstrated capability and intent to disrupt industrial control systems (ICS) at electric utilities and oil & gas facilities.

Attack Timeline

Initial reconnaissance activity detected targeting public-facing assets of multiple US electric utilities.

Technical Analysis

Initial Access

VOLTZITE primarily gains initial access through exploitation of public-facing applications, particularly VPN concentrators and remote access gateways. The threat actor has demonstrated capability to exploit both known vulnerabilities (including CVE-2024-21887 in Ivanti products) and zero-day vulnerabilities in industrial remote access solutions.

IT to OT Pivot

After establishing a foothold in corporate IT networks, VOLTZITE methodically maps network architecture to identify pathways into operational technology environments. The threat actor specifically targets jump servers, historian systems, and engineering workstations that bridge IT and OT networks. Command and control communications have been observed using encrypted channels to infrastructure at honey.scanme.sh for DNS tunneling.

‚ÑπÔ∏è ICS-Specific Tools

The threat actor has been observed using custom tools capable of interacting with Modbus, DNP3, and IEC 61850 protocols. These tools appear designed for reconnaissance rather than immediate disruption, suggesting preparation for potential future sabotage operations.

Mitigation Checklist

Immediate Actions

  • Audit and restrict remote access to OT networks
  • Implement network segmentation between IT and OT environments
  • Patch all known vulnerabilities in VPN and remote access systems
  • Enable multi-factor authentication for all remote access
  • Review and restrict use of default credentials on ICS devices
  • Monitor for anomalous Modbus, DNP3, and IEC 61850 traffic
  • Implement application allowlisting on engineering workstations
  • Establish out-of-band communication for incident response
