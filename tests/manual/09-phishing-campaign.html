Campaign Overview

Proofpoint researchers have identified a large-scale credential phishing campaign attributed to the threat actor TA577. The campaign specifically targets employees in the financial services, legal, and professional services sectors across Germany, France, Netherlands, Belgium, and the United Kingdom.

The phishing emails impersonate popular cloud services and financial institutions, directing recipients to convincing credential harvesting pages hosted on compromised infrastructure and actor-controlled domains.

Technical Analysis

Credential Harvesting Infrastructure

The threat actor utilizes a multi-layered infrastructure for credential collection. Initial landing pages are hosted on compromised legitimate websites, which then redirect victims to actor-controlled phishing pages. Harvested credentials are exfiltrated via encrypted channels to backend servers.

Analysis of compromised infrastructure revealed several enterprise systems being abused as mail relays, including hosts identified as oaev-test-linux-01 in victim network logs.

Evasion Techniques

The campaign employs several techniques to evade detection:

  • URL shorteners and redirectors to obscure final destination
  • CAPTCHA challenges to prevent automated analysis
  • Geofencing to only display phishing content to targeted regions
  • Client-side JavaScript rendering to evade email gateway scanning
