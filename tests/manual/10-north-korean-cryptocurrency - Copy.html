The Lazarus Group, a North Korean state-sponsored threat actor, has stolen over $620 million in cryptocurrency during 2024 through a series of sophisticated attacks targeting cryptocurrency exchanges, DeFi protocols, and bridge services. This report provides a comprehensive analysis of their tactics, techniques, and the complex money laundering infrastructure used to convert stolen funds.

Primary targets have included exchanges operating in South Korea, Japan, Singapore, and United States. The stolen funds are assessed to support North Korea's weapons of mass destruction (WMD) and ballistic missile programs.

‚è±Ô∏è Attack Timeline

Q2 2024

Campaign begins with targeted spear-phishing against exchange employees. LinkedIn profiles used to identify security and DevOps personnel.

July 2024

Attackers compromise employee workstations including systems identified as oaev-test-win-05 and oaev-test-win-02 at a target exchange.

August 2024

Hot wallet private keys exfiltrated. Attackers establish persistence using legitimate remote access tools. C2 traffic observed to honey.scanme.sh.

September 2024

Multiple DeFi protocols compromised through supply chain attacks. Malicious npm packages deployed.

October 2024

Linux infrastructure including oaev-test-linux-01 servers compromised at bridge operator. Private keys stolen.

November 2024

Largest single theft executed. Funds immediately routed through Tornado Cash and cross-chain swaps.
